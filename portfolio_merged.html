About

Through frameworks grounded in entropy, equilibrium, and informational constraint, I seek to uncover deep connections between seemingly disparate domains of knowledge, treating structure itself as the foundational object of inquiry.

Research & Publications

The Riemann Hypothesis as Entropic Equilibrium: Toward a Law of Science

MetaScientae submission, 2025

Reframes the Riemann Hypothesis as an equilibrium in symbolic curvature derived from compression gradients. This work proposes that the non-trivial zeros of the zeta function represent a natural balance point in the informational geometry of prime distribution.

Entropy-Constrained Knowledge Principle

PhilSci Archive, 2025

Argues that the act of knowing is an entropic trade-off between structural constraint and informational freedom. Develops a formal framework showing that knowledge acquisition necessarily involves compression and that cognitive systems operate under fundamental informational limits.

Imperfection as a Constitutive Property of AI

Philsci Archive (Under Review), 2025

Develops the notion that algorithmic imperfection is not a flaw but a necessary condition of synthetic intelligence. Demonstrates that computational systems capable of learning and adaptation must inherently operate within regimes of uncertainty and approximation.
